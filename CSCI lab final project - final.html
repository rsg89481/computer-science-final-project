Cigarettes are the number one most littered item in America.
In 2009, during the annual International Coastal Cleanup, Ocean Conservancy found that about
3,216,991 cigarettes or cigarette buts were collected from beaches and inland waterways all over the world.

Cigarettes butts are not biodegradable and are made of cellulose acetate (a natural plastic).
They break down enough to meet most cities toxic waste guidelines but will never full decompose.
Cigarette butts can leach out toxic chemicals, like arsenic and lead, into the environment which can contaminate
water and vegetation. Contaminated water can poison fish and cigarette butts have been linked to contaminated
vegetation which can and has affected the lives of livestock. In addition, they can kill small animals if ingested.
There has even been cases where small children have picked up discarded cigarettes and ingested them,
leading them to become ill. As society advances and grows, we should all try our hardest to help minimize the
detrimental impact we create every day on the earth.
